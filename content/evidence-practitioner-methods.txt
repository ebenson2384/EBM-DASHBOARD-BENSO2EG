# Practitioner Evidence: Methods & Acquisition Strategy
# Document your approach to gathering expert insights and professional experience

## Practitioner Evidence Goals
**Primary Goal:** Understand how workplace communication practices actually affect employee performance in real organizational settings from experienced HR professionals, managers, and organizational development consultants.
**Secondary Goal:** Identify practical implementation strategies and common pitfalls when improving workplace communication systems to enhance job performance.

## Target Practitioners

### Industry Experts
**Target Profile:** HR professionals, organizational development specialists, and managers with direct experience implementing communication improvement initiatives
- Years of experience desired: 10+ years in organizational management or HR
- Industry/sector focus: General business organizations (private sector, government, non-profit)
- Role/position types: HR Directors, Chief People Officers, Organizational Development Consultants, Senior Managers
- Company size experience: Mid-size to large organizations (100+ employees) where communication systems are formalized
- Geographic location: United States (to align with FEVS dataset context)

1. **Name/Title/Organization:** Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM) - Research Publications & Practitioner Reports

   - **Contact Method:** Public reports and research publications available at shrm.org/research

#### Interview Protocol
**Note:** Adapting interview questions to guide analysis of published practitioner sources

**Opening Questions (adapted for document analysis):**
- What is the practitioner's background and experience with workplace communication?
- How long have they been working in organizational development/HR/management?

**Problem-Focused Questions:**
- Have they encountered communication-performance gaps in organizations?
- What causes communication breakdowns that affect employee performance?
- How common is poor workplace communication in their experience?

**Solution-Focused Questions:**
- What communication improvement approaches have they implemented or observed?
- What solutions have they seen work effectively?
- What would they recommend based on their experience?

**Implementation Questions:**
- What challenges arise when implementing communication improvements?
- What would they do differently based on lessons learned?
- What resources or support are critical for success?

**Closing Questions:**
- What are the key takeaways from their experience?
- What evidence do they provide for their recommendations?
