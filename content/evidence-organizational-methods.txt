# Organizational Evidence: Internal Data Collection Strategy
# Document your approach to gathering internal organizational evidence

## Internal Evidence Goals
**Primary Goal:** Obtain quantitative data from FEVS (Federal Employee Viewpoint Survey) to measure current levels of workplace communication (X), employee engagement (M), and job performance (Y) within federal government organizations, establishing baseline evidence for communication-performance relationship in our target context.

**Secondary Goal:** Assess organizational capacity and readiness for communication improvement initiatives by analyzing FEVS data on management practices, resources, and barriers to effective communication; identify high-performing vs. low-performing organizations to understand success factors.

## Available Data Sources

### Performance Metrics/KPIs
**Data Source: Federal Employee Viewpoint Survey (FEVS) 2024**
- **Human Resources Data:** Employee engagement index, satisfaction scores, performance accountability measures
- **Communication Metrics:** Supervisor communication, information sharing, organizational transparency items
- **Operational Data:** Work unit performance, quality of work, productivity indicators (via proxy survey items)
- **Organizational Climate:** Trust, fairness, empowerment, innovation climate measures
- **Sample:** 600,000+ federal employees across 80+ agencies

### Internal Systems
**Primary System: FEVS Public Data File from OPM (Office of Personnel Management)**
- **Dataset:** FEVS_2024_Governmentwide_Data.xlsx
- **Access:** Publicly available download from https://www.opm.gov/fevs/public-data-file/
- **Variables:** 84 core survey items + demographic variables + engagement indices
- **Format:** Excel/CSV with aggregate statistics by agency, subagency, demographic groups
- **Documentation:** Technical reports, questionnaires, data dictionaries provided by OPM

**Supplementary Sources:**
- OPM Best Places to Work rankings (derived from FEVS)
- Federal News Network agency analyses
- Agency-specific FEVS reports and action plans

### Reports and Documentation
- **Regular Reports:** OPM Annual FEVS Technical Report with methodology and governmentwide findings
- **Survey Results:** FEVS Governmentwide Management Report showing trends 2011-2024
- **Historical Studies:** Prior year FEVS data for longitudinal trend analysis (2020-2024 available)
- **Benchmark Data:** OPM Best Places to Work rankings identifying high performers
- **Agency Reports:** Individual agency FEVS results and improvement initiatives

#### Time Period Analysis

#### Contact Plan
1. **Primary Contact:** OPM FEVS Team (fevs@opm.gov)

- **Employee Privacy:** FULLY PROTECTED - OPM aggregates data to prevent identification of individual respondents; minimum cell size of 10 respondents for any reported statistic
- **Data Security:** Public data; no special security measures required; standard data handling practices sufficient
- **Use Limitations:** Can use for academic research, policy analysis, organizational benchmarking; cannot attempt to de-anonymize or re-identify individuals
- **Reporting Boundaries:** Can share findings publicly; data already public domain; acknowledge OPM as source

## Data Analysis Plan

### Baseline Establishment
#### Pre-Problem Baselines
**Historical FEVS Communication Scores (2020-2024):**
- 2020: Supervisor communication average ~72% positive (pre-remote work)
- 2021-2022: Dip to ~70% during pandemic remote transition  
- 2023-2024: Recovery to ~74% as hybrid work normalizes
- **Interpretation:** Communication shows resilience; current 74% is improved from pandemic low but still indicates 26% of employees experience communication problems

#### Normal Operating Ranges
**Governmentwide FEVS Benchmarks (What's "Normal"):**
- **High Performers (Top Quartile Agencies):** 80-85% positive on supervisor communication items
- **Typical Performers (Middle 50%):** 70-75% positive (where governmentwide average sits)
- **Low Performers (Bottom Quartile):** 60-65% positive
- **Interpretation:** Current governmentwide 74% is "normal" but substantial room for improvement toward top performer range

#### Benchmark Comparisons
- **Government vs. Private Sector:** Gallup private sector engagement ~36%; FEVS engagement 67.7% - government higher baseline
- **Internal Benchmarks:** Compare highest-performing agencies (NASA, NTSB ~80%) vs. lowest (some departments ~60%) - 20-point spread shows communication improvement is achievable
- **Target:** Move governmentwide average from 74% toward 80% (top quartile) represents realistic but ambitious goal

### Problem Evidence Analysis
#### Problem Indicators
**Data confirming communication problem exists:**
1. Only 69.3% trust supervisor (Q61) - means 30.7% lack trust
2. Only 74.2% feel supervisor listens (Q54) - means 25.8% feel unheard
3. Engagement index 67.7% - gap of 12-15 points from top performers
4. High variance across agencies (60-85% range) - inconsistent communication quality
5. **Conclusion:** Clear evidence of widespread communication gaps affecting roughly 1 in 4 federal employees

#### Problem Severity Measurement
**Quantifying severity:**
- **Scale:** 600,000+ employee responses provide confidence in estimates
- **Impact:** Communication items among strongest correlates of engagement (r ~ .50+ based on prior FEVS research)
- **Gaps:** 20-point difference between high and low performers = substantial improvement potential
- **Trend:** Stable over 5 years (not getting worse but not improving) = persistent problem
- **Severity Rating:** MODERATE-HIGH - affects significant minority (25-30%), stable problem, proven link to engagement

#### Root Cause Investigation
**What FEVS data reveals about causes:**
- Supervisor items (Q54-Q61) lower than organizational leadership items - problem concentrated at frontline manager level
- "Provided with opportunities to demonstrate leadership skills" (Q45: 61.7%) lower than communication items - potential manager training gap
- "Results-oriented performance culture" items mixed - may reflect communication about performance expectations
- Work-life balance items (Q58-59: ~72%) correlate with communication - workload may constrain manager time for communication

### Solution Feasibility Analysis
#### Resource Assessment
**FEVS data on organizational capacity:**
- Q48 "Employees have training opportunities" 77.8% positive - training infrastructure exists
- Q52 "Organization provides resources to do job" 67.2% - resource constraints moderate but not severe
- Q67 "Senior leaders maintain high standards of honesty and integrity" 62.0% - leadership credibility modest; may affect initiative uptake
- **Interpretation:** Moderate organizational capacity; training systems in place; resource constraints acknowledged

#### Capability Assessment
**Data showing change readiness:**
- Q40 "Encouraged to come up with new ideas" 62.4% - moderate innovation culture
- Q21 "Willing to put in extra effort" 87.3% - high employee commitment despite engagement gaps
- Q70 "Leaders generate high levels of motivation" 55.0% - leadership capability gap
- **Interpretation:** Employees willing but leadership capability for change moderate; need strong change management

#### Risk Assessment
**Potential implementation barriers from FEVS:**
- Q66 "Satisfied with involvement in decisions" 53.9% - employees feel excluded; participation important for buy-in
- Q71 "Senior leaders' work location decisions" 52.5% - trust in leadership decisions lower; need transparent communication about initiative
- Historical FEVS scores stable - change inertia may be high; dramatic shifts don't happen quickly
- **Key Risk:** Manager resistance if communication improvement seen as added burden without removing other tasks

### Quality Assurance
**Ensuring data accuracy:**
- **Verification:** Cross-check downloaded data against OPM published reports for governmentwide statistics
- **Spot Checks:** Randomly verify 10+ data points match OPM technical report tables
- **Missing Data:** Document any missing values; OPM uses "X" for suppressed cells (n<10)
- **Calculation Validation:** Reproduce OPM's published composite scores (e.g., EEI) to verify understanding
- **Peer Review:** Have classmate or instructor review analysis approach before finalizing

### Version Control
**Managing data and analysis versions:**
- **/data folder:** Store original downloaded files; never modify originals
- **/analysis folder:** Separate folder for processed data and analysis outputs
- **File naming:** FEVS_2024_original.xlsx, FEVS_2024_analyzed_v1.xlsx, FEVS_2024_analyzed_final.xlsx
- **Date stamps:** Include analysis date in filenames (e.g., analysis_2025-11-21.R)
- **Change log:** Text file documenting what changed between versions and why

## Expected Challenges

### Data Availability
**Challenges encountered:**
- ✅ **RESOLVED:** FEVS data publicly available - downloaded successfully
- ⚠️ **LIMITATION:** Individual-level data not available (only aggregated) - limits sophisticated statistical analyses
- ⚠️ **LIMITATION:** Some agency-level data suppressed for small agencies (n<10) - can't analyze all organizations
- ⚠️ **LIMITATION:** Open-ended comments not fully published - miss qualitative richness

### Data Quality Issues
**Potential problems:**
- **Response rate:** FEVS 2024 ~40% response rate - non-response bias possible; satisfied employees may respond less
- **Self-report bias:** All perceptual data; no objective performance metrics paired with FEVS
- **Social desirability:** Employees may over-report positive attitudes or under-report criticism
- **Common method variance:** Communication, engagement, performance all from same survey source - inflates correlations
- **Mitigation:** Acknowledge limitations; triangulate with practitioner and scientific evidence

### Access Barriers
**Obstacles overcome:**
- ✅ **NONE:** Public data freely accessible
- **Time investment:** Significant time required to understand 168-page technical report and 84-item survey
- **Technical skills:** Need data analysis capability (Excel, R, Python) to work with large dataset
- **Interpretation expertise:** Understanding federal government context helps interpret findings appropriately

### Time Constraints
**How time affected data collection:**
- **Timeline:** 2-3 days to download, understand, and conduct basic analysis (within class project timeframe)
- **Depth trade-off:** Comprehensive analysis would take weeks; focused on key variables (communication, engagement, performance)
- **Prioritization:** Analyzed governmentwide data first; left agency-specific deep dives for future work
- **Documentation burden:** Proper documentation takes 30-40% of total time; worth investment for replicability

## Backup Plans
**Contingencies if primary approach failed:**

1. **If FEVS data unavailable:** Use published OPM reports with summary statistics rather than raw data (less flexible but sufficient for basic analysis)

2. **If download link broken:** Contact OPM directly via email (fevs@opm.gov); request data file or alternative access method

3. **If data format unusable:** Convert Excel to CSV; use online converters if software compatibility issues

4. **If analysis skills insufficient:** Simplify to descriptive statistics (means, percentages) rather than advanced regression; still provides valuable baseline evidence

5. **If interpretation uncertain:** Consult OPM technical documentation; review academic papers citing FEVS; ask instructor for guidance
